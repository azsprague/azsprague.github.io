Design Build Fly at VT - Project Manager

May 2021 - Present | Website

DBF was the first club I joined at Virginia Tech; each year, we create an unmanned, electric powered, radio-controlled aircraft to be applied to "real world" missions. It's a pretty substantial task which involves months of work and dozens of workers, so members are divided into sub-teams where they focus on specific parts of the process. As a Freshman, I joined the Manufacturing team - we were responsible for creating the physical plane from the CAD models.

Alongside Manufacturing, I shadowed the club's Student Engineer's Council Representative and Outreach Coordinator to learn more about how the club gets funding and promotes itself. Fast-Forward to the 2020-21 school year, and I was in that exact position! My duties included representing the team amongst every other engineering group, allocating funding from the SEC, organizing and filing receipts, and planning outreach events. Working closely alongside the Project Manager, I was able to get a feel for what that role included, and at the end of my Sophomore year I was elected for that position.

The year hasn't begun yet, however I've been working with the Chief Engineer (Lemuel Hook) to get the team prepared for the upcoming season. Stay tuned for more info!
